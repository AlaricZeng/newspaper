Music streaming service Deezer has revealed that nearly half of all new music uploads are now generated by artificial intelligence. The company, which has developed its own AI detection technology, reports that a staggering 44% of new tracks are AI-created, and that the vast majority of streams for these songs are fraudulent.

Deezer's findings highlight a growing challenge for the music industry as generative AI tools become more sophisticated and accessible. The company is taking a proactive stance, demonetizing approximately 85% of AI-generated music streams to combat fraudulent activity.

The report also touches on the technical side of the issue. While major platforms for AI music generation, such as Google's Lyria 3, embed digital watermarks to identify their creations, the article notes the increasing ease with which these watermarks can be removed. Furthermore, custom-built models can generate music without any watermarks at all, making detection a significant hurdle.

This influx of AI-generated content poses a number of questions for the future of music and streaming. As the lines between human and machine-created art continue to blur, the industry will need to adapt to ensure fair compensation for artists and a transparent listening experience for users.
